Success rate (horizon-scoped)

Attempts-weighted average acceptance rate across every 5-min tick in the selected horizon:

sum(success_dil + success_std) ÷ sum(attempts), summed over ticks in window.

Source: the stresstest CSV log. Each row's status is success if sendtoaddress returned a txid, or failed if it raised an RPC error.

Note: this measures mempool acceptance only — not on-chain confirmation. See the "Confirmed on-chain" card for that.

Mempool average (horizon-scoped)

Primary value = mean tx count in the local node's mempool across all 5-min health ticks in the selected horizon.

Subtitle "now X · peak Y" — the most recent snapshot (from the latest tick) and the highest value observed in the window.

Source: getmempoolinfo.size at each health tick (sampled every 5 min).

Bounded fluctuation = healthy. Rising window averages over time = blocks falling behind submissions.

Confirmed on-chain—

Confirmed on-chain

Within the selected time horizon, count of successfully submitted txs that are now included in a block.

How: every 5 min a sweep scans recent blocks (up to ~3000 back from tip) and intersects their tx lists against every success txid in the stresstest CSV log. Results are bucketed by submission time so the dashboard can scope them to any horizon.

Percent is against confirmed + pending + dropped for the window.

Confirm latency p50—

p90/p99
Confirm latency p50

Approximate median time between stresstest submission (timestamp in the CSV) and the mined block's timestamp (block.time), for confirmed txs in the selected horizon.

How: per-5-min-bucket log-spaced histogram with 9 bins (<30 s, 30–60 s, 1–2 min, 2–5 min, 5–10 min, 10–30 min, 30–60 min, 1–3 h, >3 h). The client merges buckets inside the horizon and interpolates linearly within the bin containing the percentile.

Accuracy: expect ±10–20 s at short latencies. p90 and p99 are shown in the subtitle.

Still pending—
—
Still pending

Successful submissions in the horizon that are currently in the mempool, not yet mined.

How: during the 5-min sweep, getmempoolentry <txid> is called for each submission; membership counts here.

Fresh txs are usually pending for one or two blocks before confirming — a small non-zero value is normal.

Dropped—
—
Dropped

Successful submissions in the horizon that are neither in any observed block nor in the current mempool.

How: during the sweep, if a txid isn't in any scanned block and isn't in getrawmempool, it's classified as dropped.

Zero is the healthy state. Non-zero can indicate mempool eviction (fee too low), RBF replacement, or — rarely — a chain reorg that unconfirmed the tx.

Success rate (5-min windows)

Success rate per 5-min window

Fraction of stresstest submissions accepted into the mempool, computed per 5-min window from the stresstest CSV (success / (success + failed)).

Dashed red guide at 0.3 — the alert threshold.

This is mempool-acceptance only; chain confirmation is tracked separately in the Confirmed card.

Attempts & outcomes per 5-min window

Per-window attempts and outcomes

Counts from the stresstest CSV per 5-min window:

• attempts (gray, dotted): total submissions tried
• dilithium ok (purple): accepted Dilithium txs
• standard ok (blue): accepted standard txs
• failed (red): submissions that raised an RPC error

Submission throughput (tx/s)

Submission throughput

Transactions submitted per second, averaged over each 5-min window: attempts_5m ÷ 300.

Useful for load-test experiments where you've changed the stresstest rate and want to confirm the new submission rate is sustained.

Does not reflect on-chain confirmation throughput — only what's being submitted to the wallet.

Failure breakdown per 5-min window

Failure breakdown

Failed submissions per 5-min window, split by cause. All counts come from grep-scanning the stresstest log during the 5-min health tick.

• map::at (red): the v0.3.2 sendtoaddress bug — RPC errors with code -1.
• insufficient funds (orange): wallet balance wasn't enough for the random amount selected.
• no confirmed UTXOs (yellow): no ≥1-conf outputs available to spend.
• other RPC (gray): any other RPC error.

Under load, comparing these lines tells you why the success rate dropped — wallet bug vs. balance issue vs. UTXO starvation vs. something else.

UTXOs (≥1 conf)

Confirmed UTXOs over time

Count of unspent outputs with at least 1 confirmation in the stresstest wallet, from listunspent 1.

Balloons when sends fail (change outputs pile up); drains as the wallet respends them.

Block weight (MWU) per mined block

Block weight utilization

Total weight of each mined block (megaweight units, MWU) over time, plotted by the block's own blocktime.

Source: getblock <hash> 1 (.weight), falling back to getblockstats for nodes that don't return weight in getblock.

If this chart saturates at a flat ceiling (and mempool grows at the same time), blocks are weight-capped and the chain is the bottleneck. On BTQ testnet we've observed blocks up to ~5.7 MWU.

Confirmation latency distribution

Confirmation latency distribution

Histogram of confirmation delays (submit time → block time) for confirmed txs in the selected horizon.

Bins: <30 s, 30–60 s, 1–2 min, 2–5 min, 5–10 min, 10–30 min, 30–60 min, 1–3 h, >3 h.

On a healthy testnet most txs land in the first two bins (one block). A heavy tail would indicate congestion or chain stalls during the window.

Page reloads every 60 s; underlying data is regenerated every 5 min. Source: stresstest VM btq-testnet-stresstest (asia-east1-a).
